I really liked this book and thought it was very interesting. I loved the character of Scarlett and thought she was an amazing main character. I also loved the plot of the story as it was very interesting and fun to read.
I thought the start was very good as it introduced us to the characters, Tella and Scarlett. I liked how it showed us the backstory and how they had been dreaming of going to Caraval forever. I also like how it showed us the sisters' bond and how they look out for each other.
I thought that Scarlett was a very selfless person as she was willing to sacrifice herself into a loveless marriage for her sister. I was also so happy when she finally got her invitation to Caraval as she had been sending letters there forever.
I really liked Julian and Scarlett's relationship from the start as they were so cute together. Their banter and the ways they would always look out for each other was amazing.
I liked the world of Caraval as it was such a fun game to follow and read about and I just wanted to be the one playing it.
I also liked that the game's prize was Tella's freedom. I thought this was a great twist and gave Scarlett a lot more motivation which made it more interesting for me to read.
I loved all the riddles and clues that were given as it made me motivated to try and solve it even though I couldn't. I also liked the mystery behind Legend's character and the connection he had to her family.
I loved how the lines between what was real and what was the game started to blur and how we were constantly reminded that none of it was real. I loved this magical aspect as it was so interesting to read and just gets you caught up in the world.
The final part of the game was the most shocking. I was floored when it was revealed that Julian was part of the game and I felt so bad for Scarlett. This made her question her relationship with him and every interaction because everything had been orchestrated by Legend.
I was so sad when Tella jumped off the balcony and died as I never expected this to ever happen. BUT THEN it turned out this was all a con against their abusive dad. I was shocked. My mouth was on the floor. I never expected this to happen and I was kind of mad at Tella for putting her sister through that. I do love that the novel ended with Scarlett and Tella reuniting and showing their amazing bond together. I also like how it ended on a cliff hanger and opened up perfectly for the next book.

This book was not as good as Caraval because I did not like Tella's point of view as much as Scarletts. I did really like the plot and the storyline of this book and found it a lot more magical.
I really liked the plot of this book as I found the idea of Tella finding out Legend's name in exchange for her lost mother very interesting. I loved how she got the invitation for another round of Caraval as I really enjoyed reading about Caraval in the last book.
I liked how this game had higher stakes and we were able to learn more about this magical world they live in. I thought that Fates was a very interesting concept that was written very well. I also loved the mystery around Legend and the rumours that were going around about him.
I really liked the character of Jacks as I found him really charming and had great dialogue. Though I thought the character of Dante was a better fit for Tella as they had a better connection.
I liked Tella's role in the game and how she was a key to helping out the fates. I also loved how she was on a mission to find out more about her mother. I liked the destiny cards as well as it expanded on the magical aspect and added even more layers to the book.
My favourite part was the mystery in the book with the Fates and with Legend. I also loved the character of Jack's so much throughout the book.
I loved the final revelation that Dante was actually Legend. I actually knew this was going to happen because I accidently spoiled myself which was very stupid but it was a good plot twist that I did not expect.
I thought that Tella giving up her possible relationship with Dante/Legend and the search for her mother to save the world from the fates showed her character and the development she had throughout the book.
I like how it ended by setting up for the book and showing that Tella's story is far from over.
Overall I enjoyed this book as I thought the side characters and the plot was really good but I just didn't love that it was in Tella's point of view.

I really liked this book as it had both Tella and Scarlett's point of view. I thought it was a great ending to the series and I enjoyed it a lot. I loved the magical aspect and the plot was great.
I liked the way it showed the aftermath of Caraval and Tella's conflicting feelings. I liked how it showed her feelings for both Dante and Jacks.
I was also very intrigued by Scarlett's storyline and the love triangle she had with the Count and Julian. Though I always knew it would be Julian as he is the best. I also liked the revelation of her mother and that she had been a part of the Fates and was the key to taking the other Fates down.
I really enjoyed the twist that the Fallen Star (leader of the Fates) was actually Scarlett's father. I had not expected this to happen and it was a really nicely written twist.
I thought that Tella's relationship struggles were not that interesting. I didn't hate it but it wasn't my favourite storyline. I thought it was an interesting storyline that Legend really liked Tella but refused to give up his immortality and love her. I thought it was a bit unrealistic that Tella expected that though because he didn't give up immortality to love his brother so why would he give up his immortality to someone he just met. I did love Jacks though as he is amazing and can do no wrong.
I liked how Scarlett's story was one of empowerment and self discovery. I thought she had great character development in this book but I do know that it should've been Julian the whole time.
I really liked the final battle against the Fates and thought that it was a great climax. I like how it was high stakes and packed with magic. I also liked how Legend sacrificed his immortality for Tella. I do think that it was a bit selfish of Legend to give up his immortality to someone he met not that long ago but would not give up her immortality for his own brother.
I do like the ending and how all the loose ends are wrapped up. I also loved how Scarlett chose Julian because he was always the right choice like there was never really another option. I also loved how the book ended with some lingering magic and how that opened up to further books.
